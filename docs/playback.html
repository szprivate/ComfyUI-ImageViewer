Playback Controls

The viewer treats every tab as a potential sequence. Use the timeline and playback controls to review image batches, frame-by-frame outputs, and folder-loaded sequences at any speed.

Viewer showing the playback toolbar at the bottom — transport buttons, timeline slider, FPS input, and loop mode selector.

Transport Buttons

The five transport buttons on the left of the playback toolbar control frame-level navigation:

ButtonKeyboardAction
⏮ RewindCtrl+←Jump to frame 1
◁ Step Back←Go back one frame
▶ / ⏸ Play/PauseSpaceStart or stop playback
▷ Step Forward→Advance one frame
⏭ EndCtrl+→Jump to last frame
Tip Keyboard shortcuts only activate when your mouse cursor is hovering over the viewer panel. Move your cursor inside the viewer before pressing keys.

The Timeline

The timeline slider stretches across most of the playback toolbar. It shows major and minor tick marks as visual reference. The current frame position is highlighted.

Scrubbing

Click anywhere on the timeline to jump to that frame. Click and drag to scrub through frames interactively. This is the fastest way to review a long sequence.

Selecting a Playback Sub-Range

Hold Ctrl while dragging on the timeline to define a sub-range. The selected region is highlighted in orange. Playback will loop only within this range — useful for focusing on a specific section of a long sequence without re-generating it.

Ctrl+drag on the timeline to set the orange sub-range. Playback stays within it.

To clear the sub-range and return to full-sequence playback, Ctrl+click on an empty part of the timeline outside the selection.

FPS Control

The FPS input box next to the loop mode selector sets the playback speed. The default is 25 fps. Click the number and type a new value, or scroll the mouse wheel over it.

Common values:

FPSUse case
1–4Very slow step-through for debugging individual frames
12–15Anime / stop-motion style
24–25Film / broadcast standard (default)
60Smooth game-engine style preview

Loop Modes

The loop mode dropdown controls what happens when playback reaches the end of the sequence (or sub-range):

ModeBehaviour
LoopWraps back to the first frame and continues playing.
Ping-PongReverses direction at each end — plays forwards then backwards continuously.
OnceStops at the last frame and pauses.

Working with Batches

When a bEpicSendToViewer node receives a batch of images (tensor shape [N, H, W, 3] where N > 1), all N frames are loaded into the tab automatically. The tensor shape overlay will read [N, H, W, 3] instead of [1, H, W, 3] [Still Frame].

Use the timeline or ←/→ keys to step through individual batch frames, or press Space to play them as an animation.

Frame Counter

A small frame counter is displayed on the timeline — it shows the current frame number and total frame count (e.g. 5 / 24).
